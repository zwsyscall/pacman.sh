*Just one more walk, just one more game, just one more argument with myself.*

All lies I tell myself to cope with how I feel. I hope and cope that the next thing I do that usually makes me feel good, makes me feel good this time as well. I've stopped basically all of my hobbies for the time being and I spend the majority of my time exercising, listening to music and thinking about my life, future and choices I've made. I get no enjoyment from games, shows, reading or exercise for that matter. I have no motivation to be creative. What a time to be alive!

For context, I'm going through what I would say is arguably one of the worst ruts of my life so far. It's not a unique rut or exceptionally bad insofar as everybody goes through this at least once in their lives, it just hit me differently than before.

I usually use exercise as a twofold therapeutic measure. It makes me feel good and it helps me process emotions. Now, it's just helping me work through emotions but there's no good feeling to be gained there or anywhere else for that matter. It's difficult to keep going on but I've got to, because I know that I'll get over this eventually and wasting away months of potential progress is no good. I guess an upside is that now that everyday life is very much a struggle, I've got a boost of motivation to work on things that make me exceptionally unhappy but are hard to go through. A drop in the sea doesn't feel as bad as rain on a sunny day.

The only upside I can see right now, is that I'm working through some deeper thoughts and re-evaluating what I want from life. There are going to be hard choices ahead of me, but I'd rather have them now than to wake up tomorrow at 40 and regret my life.

I feel stupid and guilty for feeling as depressed as I do now. A large part of it is context, I think. I had an incredibly dreamy few months followed by \<this\> dread, the huge drop in my feelings makes me feel more depressed than before in an almost cyclical way. In a similar sense, in some way I feel like I'm blowing what happened out of proportion but also rationally, having read my (paper) journal from the last couple of months, I know I'm also not. What I felt was real, what I feel now is real. Maybe I'll be able to look back on this post in a year or so and feel an overwhelming sense of silliness over how much I blew this out of proportion, one can only hope.

## What's next?
For the first time since I was, what, 14? I don't know. I don't know where I'll settle down, I don't know what I want from my career, I don't know what I want from my personal life. I thought through and uprooted a lot of my previously sternly held beliefs to allow myself to feel how I felt over the last couple of months, but now as a result, the future looks bleaker than it's ever looked before. I have no sense of future to cling to or to work towards. There are too many irons in the fire and the air is thick with the smell of conflict.
