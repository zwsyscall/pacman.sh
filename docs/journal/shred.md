What an edgy start to a post, waow.  
This post's main goal is to try and discuss how I feel and as such, try to derive at some form of *me*. 

I've spent a considerable amount of energy and time trying to figure out an answer to this question lately. While I don't think I'm able to be truly honest in a public setting just yet, I'm also adamant in thinking that writing about this to some depth probably helps alleviate some of the discomfort I've grown to have with who I am. Maybe I'm wrong, but being more open with myself is kind of the point of this piece.

To start, I think my interests are probably pretty set in stone. I'm sure I like what I like. I draw most of my personality from my interests, I've always been "an IT guy". This does raise an interesting point because I never really sought to figure out which way it should go. Does some form of I emerge from my interests or vice versa? Is what I like who I am? Anyways, I digress. I've got what I'd like to believe some moral axioms by which I derive what is right and wrong, but I'm far from perfect and absolutely "vibe out" moral beliefs from time to time. I don't know if it's necessarily the case that a person needs a fully fleshed out moral framework, because society largely grants you a framework by which to determine what is right and wrong <sub><sub>1</sub></sub> It's also kind of interesting how there are some morals truths that seem to transcend time and space. Some might say it's an objective moral truth, I probably beg to differ. Then again, I haven't spent enough to really think it through -- nor do I care to.

As a sidenote to a sidenote, I really enjoy this line of writing where I don't actually have to stay on topic and I'm able to stream my consciousness. It leads me to interesting topics that I can bookmark so I can ponder on them at a later date.

While my interests are all nerd stuff like programming, osint, surfing da webz, a bit of playing games in some form or another, I don't know if I really fit the stereotype of "a nerd". The point here isn't to make myself out to be different and deep while other people are shallow and boring, but more so try to point at societal archeotypes that I probably should<sub><sub>2</sub></sub> relate to. I've always felt out of place at any hobbies, school or even the main online groups I frequent. Heck, even at the one place where I was arguably most among peers in every sense of the word, I still felt like the odd one out. I feel like parts of my identity clash hard enough with the "standard" in any place, that it's hard to feel at place anywhere. Maybe this is quintessential human experience, but I feel like I should have found \<some\>, \<any\> place I really feel like I fit in by now. 

Thus far throughout my life, I've externally conformed to society's norms. I've been a "normal looking" gay<sub><sub>3</sub></sub> guy. I went to the school that was expected of me, I got to the university that was expected of me for, I started hobbies that were expected of me. As my closest friends IRL know, I like to 'joke' about my high school and universities because it's a parody in on itself to talk about them consistently, as if they somehow define me. I never really felt any true pride about going to the school(s) I went to, but it was expected of me to be vocal and proud and as such, I was. Aside from my interests that you'd never know about unless we talked, I did not stand out. I should note that I did not perform well in school for a couple of reasons, but that's neither here or there.

Like I've said a few times in other writings, I think this era of conformity is coming to an end and I have to start living my life the way I want. I ought not do what's expected of me for the sake of doing it, it's my life and I should live it the way I want. If this means doing stupid shit and getting burnt for it, that's fine. I at least owe it to myself to be who I want. Much like I've stated in another post, I detest the idea of waking up at 40 only to regret all the choices I did just to conform to these meaningless norms which only serve me to hide in plain sight. Truthfully, I also don't think I'll find happiness or partnership by "living a lie" like I have, even if I'm so very used to the masking by now. 

During my last relationship, I was *much* more open about every aspect of myself than I've ever been with another person. To actually open up about my deepest feelings for the first time and simultaniously be understood and accepted opened up a pandora's box of "what ifs"<sub><sub>4</sub></sub>. This is and the relationship coming to an abrupt end have largely fueled this introspection because I am not happy with who I am, simply put. I do not look at myself and think "yeah that's who I am". I've always felt this way but up until now, I've just kind of pushed it away. In the words of Gösta Sundqvis, [*Olen nyt jo turtunut tuskan tunteeseen*](https://youtu.be/PQkn1MfMWB8?si=3wBYXCdQoTfFSb_P&t=130). <sub><sub>*I've already become numb to the feeling of agony*</sub></sub>
