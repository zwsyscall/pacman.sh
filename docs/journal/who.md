This post mainly contains my view on certain prosocial behaviours and ways I've tried to improve as a person. Very autistic, I'm aware. Much like journalctl, this post aims to be a "forever" post, where I update this over time.

Growing up, I was an antisocial person. Even up to few years ago I was still partly an antisocial person (albeit different). I was angry at the world and at people for not thinking about things the way I do, seeing myself above everybody else. *My* opinions were better and fully thought through but *your* opinions were barely worth considering. If someone disagreed with me with enough conviction, it was a perceived slight at me. I wanted to double down on my beliefs even when confronted with opposing facts. This is a *terrible* way to live, not only was I miserable but I was also terrible company. I'd like my emotions get the better of me in minor ways, I'd end up being snarky and rude to people. I cringe at  \<most\> of this behaviour and wish I could take it back. <sub><sub> (Editors note: some of the behaviour was justified)</sub></sub> 

I am by no means good with these things, but I'm better than I was growing up and better than I was yesterday. That's what matters. Comparing yourself to other people is kind of silly, even though everyone does it (me included). 

### Mental framing
During my teenage years I started thinking more about people, my loneliness and why I was the way I was. This started a long journey of thinking through my opinions more carefully and trying to be more empathetic to other people and their experiences. One of the earliest, smallest things I did was change how I phrase things and how I frame things in my mind. I would *not* make overly strong statements and I started avoiding tying my ego with my beliefs. This was as simple as changing phrasing from: `I know for sure that the earth is flat` to 
`From what I've seen, the earth is flat`. Now when I come to new information that contradicts my view, the framing doesn't tie in my identity, my validity or me to the statement's factuality. The mental framing made taking accountability and being a dynamic person not only possible but also easier.

### Understanding
The second most important change I've done is become `understanding-pilled`. One of my closest friends hounded me about this for a while, about how my black and white view of people is a bad thing. I of course, being a prideful jerk, did not take this to heart. Only after reading about this in a book did I embrace it and try to take it to heart. If you're not familiar with being `understanding-pilled`, it's avoiding jumping to negative connotations based on people's behaviours. Your long time coworker snapped at your for a mistake? Maybe they just got some bad news. Someone you're meeting for the first time was rude to you? Maybe they haven't slept. You don't know so why assume the worst? This has lead me to become a way more understanding and generally nicer person. Not only am I outwardly nicer, but I also feel better. I don't start disliking someone for a perceived slight, because maybe they didn't realise it. Obviously this doesn't go on till the end of the earth and you notice patterns, but trying to be understanding goes a long way.
Before I became `understanding-pilled`, I was very much a "I wouldn't be polite at my first meeting, you are bad" -kind of person. That's no good! I only wish I listened to my friend sooner :\)


### Listening
A big problem I had before and still do to this day, is occasionally I go into passive listening mode and just zone out when another person is talking. This is rude, awkward and puts me off-beat in a conversation. I *really* dislike this behaviour from me and I'm trying to change it. A way I initially tricked myself to listen better, was to try to always come up with 2-3 good questions to someone's response. I might not ask any of them, but it forces me to actively listen. It's a habit you get good at explicitly and tricks like this helped me get better at it initially. Like I said, I still struggle and notice myself drifting off every now and then but I'm getting better, it's a marathon not a sprint.
