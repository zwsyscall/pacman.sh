# journalctl

This entry serves as an 'ever updating' journal entry on my exercising to at least try to keep me accountable. Knowing me I'll probably keep this up for a month at best but y'know, if you never try, you never do succeed.

At the time of writing, I am in a pit of depression. This has motivated me to take up walking, proper. Even before I fell into this momentary rut, I had been walking but never as seriously as this. My current walking / marching routine works N fold:
- Burns a lot of calories
- It helps break in my new boots
- I can prepare for upcoming long-distance marches
- I work through my emotions and think about things
- Even if I have a boring day, at the end of the day, I can say that I achieved \<something\>

## Thoughts

### 05.07.2026
Right now, I'm walking for as long as I want/need a day. I've also got a walking mat now, which helps cut out the hours of the day spent on explicitly walking. I can walk while I work or do stuff on the puter. My current routine is to do walking Monday - Saturday, then take Sunday off. On my walking mat, I'm averaging \~`5.5km/h`, which is around `11min/km` (quick maths). This is an okay speed, I definitely get sweaty when it's warm in / outdoors after a while, which is to be expected. If I walk outside, my average pace is \~`9.4min/km`, which is `6.3km/h`. This is way faster, but I'm tall so it's not that surprising. Neither of these speeds is taking into account breaks, this is the average walking speed when I am actually walking.

My current "plan" is to hit `100km` - `110km` a week and then after that, try to lift it up to consistently hitting `20km` a day. I can do `20km` a day right now, but I think if I increase the load slowly I'm more likely to keep up with the routine. I want to hit `40km` in one day at some point, as a once per week "treat". 
This is hard not just because of the physical challenge but also because of the time it takes to walk this distance. I think the furthest I've walked in one go in the past two years is around `26km`, although that was a weighted march so it's hardly comparable to walking on a mat while working.

I'm kind of annoyed at how bad the walking spaces in Helsinki are. Like sure, if you compare it to other cities, esp capitals, it's *really* nice. However comparing walking in Helsinki to walking at a cottage, it's just completely different. In Helsinki, you have to work to dodge the big streets and it's a monotonous place that sucks the life out of you. There are nice places to walk, but they take time to get to so it increases the threshold of going there to walk. Even when you do go there, being able to walk >`15km` *without* hitting a big road is a challenge and a half. You need to either walk in circles or otherwise plan your trip beforehand. 

I've *really* enjoyed my new boots. I got ordered the Aipi 2.2 and they're not only a treat to walk on, but also look great! I got the brown ones and I'm already considering getting the black ones, although one size smaller. The current ones I got are an EU 45, my size and fit me perfectly with boot socks. If I got the black ones, I'd get them a size smaller so they'd be perfect in everyday wear without boot socks. I don't regret my size 45 ones, because I actually want to use boot socks on long treks and they are also nearly perfect with gel insoles. (which is how I'm running them right now)
